What is the equity multiplier?
The equity multiplier is a financial ratio that measures the ammount of a company's assets that are financed through shareholder's equity rather than by debt.
How to calculate equity multiplier?
To calculate equity multiplier you should use the following formula:
equity multiplier = average total assets / average total shareholdr's equity
example: company x has total assets of $7,800,000 and $1,700,000 worth of shareholdr's equity, calculate the equity multiplier?
equity multiplier = average total assets / average total shareholdr's equity
equity multiplier = $7,800,000 / $1,700,000
equity multiplier = 4.5
Why use equity multiplier?
The equity multiplier is used by investors to measure how laveraged a fimr is. It is also used to reveal the amount of total assets that are financed by shareholdr's equity.
